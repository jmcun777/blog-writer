# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Briefing-Cascade for Foreign Employers

## Executive pulse (30 seconds)
- China termination is not at-will; legal route validity is mandatory but insufficient.
- Highest avoidable risk remains route/evidence/payroll misalignment under deadline pressure.
- Use a cascade model: leadership signal -> manager action -> payroll/legal lock check -> closure evidence gate.

## Plain-language legal context first
China employer-initiated termination generally requires a lawful statutory basis under the Labor Contract Law framework, plus process evidence that can withstand labor dispute scrutiny. Core legal anchors:
- Labor Contract Law
- Decree No. 535 implementation regulations
- SPC labor-dispute interpretation

## Cascade layer 1: Leadership decisions (what to decide now)
1. Confirm single lawful route narrative.
2. Require evidence integrity before employee-facing action.
3. Enforce pre-lock payroll/legal reconciliation.
4. Block closure without complete evidence packet.

## Cascade layer 2: Operational controls (what teams execute)
- HR: chronology integrity + script discipline.
- Legal: route consistency + settlement interpretation.
- Payroll: component-to-code mapping + tax alignment.
- Compliance: local validation + closure archive quality.

## Cascade layer 3: Trigger thresholds (when to stop)
- Route conflict -> immediate hold.
- Evidence contradiction -> pause until reconciled.
- Payroll mismatch near lock -> no-go until co-signed correction.
- Missing closure evidence -> keep case open.

## Why this is useful for foreign employers
This format mirrors real governance: concise board-level signals cascade into specific frontline controls. It reduces handoff ambiguity for non-local teams and improves response speed.

## What improved this run structurally
Compared with prior compass format, this run improves handoff clarity by explicitly separating executive decisions from operator execution and stop triggers.

## Required minimum packet
- Canonical route memo
- Versioned chronology log
- Script acknowledgement
- Settlement mapping sheet
- Payroll/tax reconciliation sheet
- City validation memo
- Closure evidence packet
