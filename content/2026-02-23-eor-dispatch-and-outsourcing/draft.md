# Labor Dispatch vs Service Outsourcing in China: EOR Buyers’ Compliance Checklist

## Executive summary
- Labor dispatch and service outsourcing are legally distinct and should not be mixed in contracts.
- Misclassification can create co-employment and penalty risk.
- Foreign employers should map real management control against contract design.

## What changed
Regulatory enforcement and practical case handling continue to scrutinize whether an arrangement is true outsourcing or de facto dispatch. Labels alone do not determine compliance; actual control and management patterns matter.

## Why it matters for foreign employers / EOR buyers
For multinational teams, structure mistakes can affect:
- employment liability allocation,
- social insurance and tax handling,
- dispute defense position.

## What to do now
- Run a role-by-role classification review.
- Align statement of work, supervision model, and payment logic.
- Avoid contract language that conflicts with real management behavior.
- Document who directs daily work and who bears employment obligations.

## FAQ
### Can we call everything outsourcing for simplicity?
No. Authorities may recharacterize based on substance over form.

### Does EOR solve dispatch classification issues automatically?
Not automatically. Design and execution still require compliant structure.

### Which team should own this review?
Legal, HR, and payroll operations should review jointly.
