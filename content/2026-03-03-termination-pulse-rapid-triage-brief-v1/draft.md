# China Employee Termination: Rapid Triage Brief for Foreign Employers (Hourly Testing Run)

## Key takeaways
- Foreign employers should run China termination decisions through a rapid triage sequence, not route-only review.
- Most avoidable failures are detectable early if teams separate legal fit, evidence fit, and payout/communication fit.
- Pulse-style triage briefing improves speed and readability for non-China decision-makers.
- Legal route and execution readiness are distinct gates; both must be green.
- Legal depth should sit in an appendix while practical action leads.

## Pulse lead
This run rotates to a **rapid triage** format: three short passes that force teams to stop preventable errors before they become disputes.

## Plain-language legal/regulatory context first
China’s Labor Contract Law sets termination boundaries and severance obligations. State Council implementation regulations and SPC interpretation shape dispute outcomes by testing procedural fairness, documentary reliability, and consistency between rationale and execution records. Practical implication for foreign operators: route logic, evidence chronology, communication behavior, and settlement execution must remain coherent through closure.

## 3-pass rapid triage model

### Pass 1: Legal route triage (Can we still use this path?)
- validate route assumptions against latest facts
- re-check protected-status and route prerequisites
- hold if assumptions changed and legal gate not refreshed

### Pass 2: Evidence and communication triage (Can we defend what we will say?)
- verify chronology completeness and contradiction log
- confirm script lock and manager briefing evidence
- block employee-facing actions if core contradictions or script drift risk persists

### Pass 3: Settlement and closure triage (Can we finish cleanly?)
- align settlement wording with payroll mapping
- unify tax explanation note
- confirm authority coverage and proof-based closure requirements

## Practical implications for foreign employers
- **HQ:** request triage outcomes, not route-only summaries.
- **Regional HR:** unresolved critical triage items should auto-trigger no-go.
- **Payroll/finance:** co-own Pass 3 before commitments.
- **Managers:** script discipline remains compliance-critical.

## Daily SOP (rapid triage)
1. Run Pass 1-3 at cycle start.
2. Assign owner/SLA for open critical items.
3. Hold execution on unresolved critical items.
4. Record go/hold/escalate decision with rationale.
5. Re-run triage on material fact changes.
6. Close only when proof-based closure gate passes.

## FAQ
### Is rapid triage legally required?
No. It is an operational governance model.

### Can small teams use this?
Yes. Start with Pass 1 and Pass 2.

### Why useful for foreign clients?
It translates legal complexity into immediate operating priorities.

### Is negotiated termination always safer?
Not automatically; execution coherence remains decisive.

### When should external PRC counsel be mandatory?
High-risk unilateral routes, protected-status concerns, restructuring, and multi-city complexity.
