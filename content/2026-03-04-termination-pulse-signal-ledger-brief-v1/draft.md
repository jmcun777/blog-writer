# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Signal-Ledger Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- China termination is not at-will; route legality is required but not sufficient.
- Use a signal ledger to track risk signal -> owner -> fix -> status.
- Highest avoidable failures remain route/evidence/payroll misalignment.
- Legal depth supports decisions as appendix, not lead narrative.

## Plain-language legal context first
China employer-initiated termination generally requires lawful statutory basis under the Labor Contract Law framework, plus coherent evidence and procedural consistency. Core anchors: Labor Contract Law, Decree No. 535 implementation rules, and SPC labor-dispute interpretation.

## Pulse signal ledger (today)

| Signal | Severity | Owner | Required action | Status rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Route rationale conflict | Critical | Legal + HR lead | Freeze outward action, issue canonical route memo | Must be closed before communication |
| Evidence chronology break | Critical | HR Ops + Compliance | Reconcile timeline/version conflicts | Must be closed before progression |
| Script drift | High | HRBP + manager lead | Re-brief and acknowledge script | Monitor until stable |
| Settlement/payroll mismatch | Critical | Payroll lead + Legal | Pre-lock reconciliation and co-sign | No-go until resolved |
| Local interpretation gap | High | Local counsel + Compliance | Complete city-level validation memo | Block final action |
| Closure evidence gap | Medium-High | Ops + Compliance | Complete payment-proof archive packet | Keep case open |

## Why this helps foreign employers
The ledger format gives board-to-operator traceability: what triggered concern, who owns it, what changed, and whether the case is safe to move. It reduces handoff ambiguity across time zones.

## What improved vs prior run format
Compared with the briefing-cascade structure, this run improves auditability and repeatability by making every control item stateful and owner-traceable.

## Required minimum packet
- Canonical route memo
- Versioned chronology log

- Settlement mapping sheet

- City validation memo
