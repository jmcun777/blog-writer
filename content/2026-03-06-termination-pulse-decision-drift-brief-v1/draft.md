# China Employee Termination 2026: Pulse Brief on Decision Drift for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- In China terminations, many failures come from **decision drift**: initial decisions are valid, then assumptions shift without formal reset.
- Foreign employers should monitor drift across route, evidence, payroll/tax, communication, and closure.
- Drift is dangerous because teams still think they are executing the original approved plan.
- If drift is detected in critical areas, pause and re-approve before communication.
- This brief is Pulse-style impact-first; legal depth is in a secondary appendix.

## What changed and why this matters now
Most multinational teams start cases with solid controls. Problems appear later when facts, timelines, or stakeholders change and the plan quietly diverges. In live China execution, this “silent drift” often causes inconsistent communication, payout confusion, and weaker defensibility.

This run rotates to a **decision-drift format** (different from previous latency/transfer/window structures). It helps non-local operators identify and correct drift before it becomes dispute exposure.

## Plain-language legal context first
China employer-initiated termination generally requires lawful route and fact-consistent execution. For foreign operators, consistency over time is essential: it is not enough to be right at intake. You also need to stay aligned as facts evolve.

Because local interpretation can vary by city, drift checks should include local-fit revalidation when material facts change.

## Pulse decision-drift board

## Drift Point 1 — Route drift
**What drifts:** legal route assumptions no longer match latest facts.

**Early signal:** new facts added, route memo unchanged.

**Correction:** re-run route fit review and update approvals.

## Drift Point 2 — Evidence drift
**What drifts:** chronology changes but contradiction log is outdated.

**Early signal:** manager updates conflict with prior records.

**Correction:** reconcile records and refresh evidence baseline.

## Drift Point 3 — Payroll/tax drift
**What drifts:** payout/IIT assumptions evolve without synchronized approval.

**Early signal:** worksheet versions diverge near notice timing.

**Correction:** freeze communication until final payroll lock is reapproved.

## Drift Point 4 — Communication drift
**What drifts:** messaging moves beyond approved script scope.

**Early signal:** unscripted manager language appears in prep.

**Correction:** re-brief spokesperson and reissue approved script pack.

## Drift Point 5 — Closure drift
**What drifts:** closure status marked complete without updated traceability.

**Early signal:** archive index missing latest decision changes.

**Correction:** rerun closure audit and retrieval test.

## Why this model helps foreign operators
Drift detection is practical for distributed teams: it catches quality decay after initial approvals. It also gives leadership a better signal than static “green” status updates.

For HQ teams, drift metrics show where process control weakens under pressure and where targeted support is needed.

## Practical scenarios

### Scenario A: route approved, then facts shift
A late fact update changes defensibility assumptions, but route approval remains unchanged.

**Outcome:** Drift Point 1 active.

**Action:** pause downstream tasks and revalidate route.

### Scenario B: payroll version drift near notice
Finance and payroll files show different payout assumptions.

**Outcome:** Drift Point 3 active.

**Action:** enforce no-notice hold until one approved version is locked.

### Scenario C: communication drift after legal sign-off
Manager adds non-approved language during preparation.

**Outcome:** Drift Point 4 active.

**Action:** re-brief and recertify communication control.

## Daily operations SOP (drift governance)

## Step 1 — Set baseline decisions at intake
- Capture approved route, evidence, payroll, and communication baselines.
- Assign drift owners by domain.

## Step 2 — Run drift checks at every transition
- Compare current state vs baseline.
- Flag material deviations.

## Step 3 — Apply drift hold rules
- Critical drift blocks communication.
- Escalate unresolved drift per SLA.

## Step 4 — Re-approve corrected baselines
- Update decision records and version history.
- Confirm downstream alignment.

## Step 5 — Close with drift audit
- Verify no unresolved drift remains.
- Capture repeat drift patterns for process fixes.

## Required document checklist
- Route memo + fallback route
- Contract and amendment records
- Chronology ledger + contradiction log
- Local interpretation note
- Severance/IIT/social worksheet (single approved version)
- Payment approvals + proof plan
- Script pack + spokesperson brief
- Closure memo + archive index + retrieval test
- Drift log (domain, baseline, change, owner, status)

## Exception handling playbook
- **Route drift near notice:** automatic hold + legal escalation.
- **Evidence drift unresolved beyond SLA:** evidence taskforce activation.
- **Payroll drift in final window:** no-communication enforcement.
- **Communication drift event:** immediate script recertification.
- **Closure drift detected post-mark-complete:** reopen case and rerun retrieval audit.

## System implementation notes
Implement baseline snapshots and drift alerts in workflow tools. Track version changes for route, evidence, payroll worksheet, and communication scripts. Add automation so communication cannot proceed if unresolved drift exists in route/evidence/payroll domains.

Track monthly KPIs: drift incidents by domain, time-to-correction, and dispute correlation.

## Common mistakes and prevention
- **Mistake:** assuming initial approval stays valid after fact changes.  
  **Prevention:** mandatory drift checks at each transition.
- **Mistake:** multiple payroll versions without final authority marker.  
  **Prevention:** single-source-of-truth lock before notice.
- **Mistake:** treating script changes as minor.  
  **Prevention:** formal communication recertification on edits.
- **Mistake:** no drift audit at closure.  
  **Prevention:** end-of-case drift clearance gate.

## FAQ

### 1) What is decision drift?
When current execution diverges from approved assumptions without formal re-approval.

### 2) Why is drift risky?
It creates inconsistency between legal logic and actual execution.

### 3) Which drift type is most sensitive?
Payroll/tax drift close to communication timing.

### 4) Can we proceed with minor drift?
Only if formally assessed and accepted; default for critical drift is hold.

### 5) How often should drift checks run?
At every major transition and on any material fact change.

### 6) Does drift control slow operations?
It usually prevents costly rework and post-notice escalation.

### 7) Is this legal advice?
No. It is an operational governance model.
