# China Minimum Wage Adjustments: What EOR Clients Should Monitor in 2026

## Executive summary
- Minimum wage standards in China are set locally, not as one national number.
- Updates can affect base salary design, probation pay, and overtime calculations.
- Foreign employers using EOR structures should track city-level notices quarter by quarter.

## What changed
In recent policy cycles, multiple provinces and municipalities have adjusted monthly and hourly minimum wage standards. The practical impact is that payroll teams must validate legal minimums at the city where employees are contractually registered and where labor is actually performed.

## Why it matters for foreign employers / EOR buyers
For cross-border employers, minimum wage is not only a hiring floor. It can also affect:
- social insurance reporting consistency,
- leave and absence pay calculations,
- labor dispute exposure when salary structures are split into fixed and variable components.

## What to do now
- Build a city-level wage threshold matrix and review monthly.
- Re-check probation salary rules against local minimum wage percentages.
- Confirm payroll vendor/EOR has a formal legal-update monitoring process.
- Keep signed amendment records when changing compensation terms.

## FAQ
### Is there one national minimum wage in China?
No. Standards are issued by local governments.

### If we pay above market, can we ignore minimum wage updates?
No. Compliance still requires meeting local legal baselines and correct payroll calculations.

### Should remote employees follow HQ city rates?
Not always. Local enforcement can consider the employee's work location and contract setup.
