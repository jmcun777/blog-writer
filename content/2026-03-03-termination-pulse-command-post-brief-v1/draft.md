# China Employee Termination: Pulse Command-Post Brief for Foreign Employers (Hourly Testing Run)

- For foreign employers, China termination execution is safer when managed through a command-post model: one situation map, one owner grid, and one escalation path.
- Most avoidable failures still come from coordination gaps between legal, HR, manager, and payroll teams.
- A command-post format turns legal context into immediate action priorities for non-local leadership.
- Negotiated exits can reduce confrontation risk, but only if settlement drafting, payroll treatment, and communication records remain aligned.
- City-level implementation variance remains material; local validation must be built into final release control.

## Situation baseline (plain-language legal context)

China is not an at-will termination market. Employer-initiated termination should align with a lawful route under the Labor Contract Law framework and be executed with coherent evidence and consistent process conduct. For foreign teams, the practical sequence remains:

1. Confirm legal route.
2. Confirm evidence quality for that route.
3. Confirm communication and settlement consistency.
4. Confirm payroll and local-validation readiness.
5. Confirm closure evidence completeness.

State Council implementation regulations and SPC labor-dispute interpretation are operationally relevant because disputes often turn on consistency across these controls.

## Pulse command-post map: 6 active sectors

### Sector 1 — Route sector

**Status question:** is there one canonical legal route rationale across all teams?  
**Escalation trigger:** route wording drift or unsupported rationale.  
**Immediate action:** hold case progression and reissue route note.

### Sector 2 — Evidence sector

**Status question:** is chronology coherent and ownership clear?  
**Escalation trigger:** material contradiction or missing ownership chain.  
**Immediate action:** freeze employee-facing steps pending reconciliation.

### Sector 3 — Communication sector

**Status question:** is script delivery aligned with approved version?  
**Escalation trigger:** script drift in manager/HR channels.  
**Immediate action:** enforce script lock and deviation approvals.

### Sector 4 — Settlement sector

**Status question:** do legal terms, payroll mapping, and manager explanation match?  
**Escalation trigger:** cross-channel mismatch.  
**Immediate action:** enforce single settlement source sheet and revalidation.

### Sector 5 — Payroll sector

**Status question:** are treatment assumptions fully validated before lock?  
**Escalation trigger:** unresolved logic near payroll cutoff.  
**Immediate action:** activate legal-payroll stop/go checkpoint.

### Sector 6 — Local + closure sector

**Status question:** has local validation passed and closure evidence completed?  
**Escalation trigger:** unresolved city caveat or incomplete proof/archive pack.  
**Immediate action:** hold release/closure until both pass.

## Practical implications for foreign employers

For non-China executives, the command-post model reduces decision lag by replacing broad status language with sector-based readiness signals. Leaders can intervene where it matters most without unpacking long procedural narratives.

For operating teams, this model reduces rework risk at critical moments such as employee communication and payroll lock. It also improves cross-entity consistency by standardizing control language and escalation timing.

## Daily command cycle (20 minutes)

### Minute 0-4: sector status reset
- Refresh Sector 1-2 status.
- Mark high-risk cases for immediate hold if needed.

### Minute 4-8: communication/settlement review
- Validate Sector 3-4 alignment.
- Record deviations and ownership.

### Minute 8-12: payroll/local readiness
- Validate Sector 5-6 before release decisions.
- Apply stop/go where unresolved.

### Minute 12-16: closure watch
- Confirm evidence pack status on near-complete cases.

### Minute 16-20: leadership broadcast
- Report only unresolved sectors with owner + ETA + next check.

## SOP by role

### HR
1. Maintain command-post board and owner map.

3. Log employee communication records.

### Legal
1. Approve route rationale and key legal wording.
2. Validate settlement legal coherence.
3. Co-sign high-risk stop/go decisions.

1. Provide date-stamped factual updates.
2. Use approved script only.
3. Escalate material changes immediately.

### Payroll/Finance
1. Validate settlement mapping and treatment assumptions.
2. Enforce pre-lock readiness criteria.
3. Produce payment proof for closure.

### Compliance/Ops
1. Audit sector transitions and reason codes.
2. Track repeated failure patterns by city/team.
3. Verify closure-file integrity.

## Required document checklist

- Canonical route note
- Evidence chronology log
- Approved script version
- Settlement source sheet
- Payroll treatment mapping
- Local validation memo
- Payment proof + closure archive checklist

SLA baseline:
- route note within 1 business day,
- chronology pass within 2 business days,
- settlement/payroll sync before lock,
- closure archive within 2 business days after payment.

## Exception playbook

### Route sector unresolved
- Stop progression.
- Reassess legal route on updated facts.
- Reissue route note before restart.

### Evidence sector unresolved
- Freeze communication.
- Resolve chronology/ownership conflict.
- Resume after verified pass.

### Settlement sector unresolved
- Use documented clarification path.
- Reconcile legal text + payroll mapping.
- Reapprove communication language.

### Payroll sector unresolved
- Trigger stop/go before lock.
- Correct and revalidate mapping.
- Align explanation language.

### Local/closure sector unresolved
- Hold release or closure.
- Complete local validation and proof/archive checks.
- Re-open status review only after completion.

## Common mistakes and prevention

### Mistake: “all green” reported without evidence
**Prevention:** require owner-confirmed proof per sector.

### Mistake: mixed document versions across teams
**Prevention:** single-source policy for route/script/settlement docs.

### Mistake: escalation without accountable owner
**Prevention:** owner + ETA required for every unresolved sector.

### Mistake: local validation appended too late
**Prevention:** include in sector 6 pre-release gate.

### Mistake: closure marked at payment event only
**Prevention:** require proof + archive completion before closure.

## FAQ

### 1) Is this legal advice?
No. This is an operations governance brief for decision support.

### 2) Can unilateral routes still be used?
Potentially yes, where legal/factual conditions support them; evidence quality remains decisive.

### 3) Why include payroll before final legal close?
Because settlement-treatment alignment can materially affect risk and employee relations.

### 4) Is mutual termination always lower risk?
No. It can help, but only with coherent execution.

### 5) What KPI should leadership start with?
Track **sector-clearance rate at release gates** and **time-to-resolve unresolved sectors**.
