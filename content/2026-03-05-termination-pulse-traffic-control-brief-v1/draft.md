# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Traffic-Control Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- Use a traffic-control model to turn legal risk into clear go/slow/stop actions.
- Route, evidence, and payroll alignment should be treated as stop-light controls.
- Lead with legal context first, then assign immediate operational implications.

## Plain-language legal context first
China is not at-will. Employer-initiated termination generally requires a lawful statutory basis plus coherent evidence and process execution under the Labor Contract Law framework. Core anchors: Labor Contract Law, Decree No. 535 implementation rules, and SPC labor-dispute interpretation.

## Pulse traffic-control board

### Red light (stop now)
- Route rationale inconsistency
- Evidence chronology contradiction
- Settlement/payroll/tax mapping mismatch

**Rule:** Any red-light item blocks progression.

### Yellow light (slow + fix)
- Manager script drift
- City-level validation pending

**Rule:** Yellow converts to red near payroll lock or employee-facing action if unresolved.

### Green light (proceed with controls)
- Route memo validated
- Evidence reconciled
- Payroll/legal co-sign complete
- Closure packet complete

**Rule:** Closure only when final green controls pass.

## Why this helps foreign employers
Traffic-control language is intuitive for cross-border teams: stop, slow, proceed. It reduces ambiguity in fast-moving cases and improves escalation speed.

## What improved vs previous run
Compared with route-map framing, this run improves real-time decisioning by translating checkpoint logic into immediate stop/slow/go actions.
