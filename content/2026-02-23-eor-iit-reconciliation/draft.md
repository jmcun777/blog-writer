# China IIT Annual Reconciliation: A Practical Guide for Foreign Employers Using EOR

## Executive summary
- Annual Individual Income Tax (IIT) reconciliation affects employee tax outcomes even with compliant monthly withholding.
- Foreign employers should coordinate payroll data quality with EOR providers before reconciliation season.
- Communication and document readiness reduce employee escalations.

## What changed
China continues annual IIT reconciliation administration under established STA frameworks. In practice, employers and EOR teams should ensure withholding records, special additional deductions, and one-off payment handling are consistently documented.

## Why it matters for foreign employers / EOR buyers
For overseas companies, reconciliation season is a recurring employee-experience and compliance moment:
- payroll data errors create trust issues quickly,
- deduction treatment questions increase support load,
- delayed clarification can trigger unnecessary disputes.

## What to do now
- Conduct a pre-season payroll data quality check.
- Confirm treatment rules for bonuses and allowances.
- Publish a short employee FAQ in English and Chinese.
- Establish an escalation path for complex tax residency questions.

## FAQ
### If monthly withholding is correct, do employees still reconcile annually?
In many cases, yes—depending on personal situation and deduction claims.

### Should foreign companies provide tax filing advice directly?
Prefer a compliance-safe approach: provide process guidance and direct legal/tax interpretation to qualified advisors.

### Does EOR remove IIT obligations?
EOR supports execution, but regulatory obligations still require proper process and controls.
