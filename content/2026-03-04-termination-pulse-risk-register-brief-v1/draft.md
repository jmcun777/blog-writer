# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Risk-Register Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- Use a live risk register to manage China termination decisions, not static checklists.
- Lead with legal route clarity, then control evidence, communication, payroll, and closure risk.
- Route/evidence/payroll misalignment remains the highest-value intervention zone.

## Plain-language legal context first
China is not an at-will termination system. Employer-initiated termination generally requires a lawful statutory basis under the Labor Contract Law framework, supported by coherent evidence and defensible process execution. Core anchors: Labor Contract Law, Decree No. 535 implementation rules, and SPC labor-dispute interpretation.

## Why this helps foreign employers
A risk register gives cross-border teams one clear operating view: what is unresolved, who owns it, and which item can block execution. This improves decision quality for leaders unfamiliar with local procedure detail.

## What improved vs previous run
Compared with signal-ledger framing, this run improves prioritization discipline by adding likelihood/impact fields and explicit escalation triggers per risk row.

## Minimum documentation controls
- Canonical route memo
- Versioned chronology log
- Script acknowledgement record
- Settlement mapping sheet
- Payroll/tax reconciliation sheet
- City-level validation memo
- Closure evidence packet
