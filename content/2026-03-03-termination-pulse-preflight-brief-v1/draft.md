# China Employee Termination: Pulse Preflight Brief for Foreign Employers (Hourly Testing Run)

- For foreign employers, China termination quality improves when each case runs through a strict preflight check before any employee-facing or payroll-lock action.
- Most costly failures still come from launch-before-ready behavior across legal, HR, manager, and payroll handoffs.
- A preflight format gives leaders a clear go/no-go decision model in minutes.
- Negotiated exits can reduce friction, but only when settlement drafting, payroll handling, and communication records stay synchronized.
- City-level implementation differences remain material; local validation should be a mandatory preflight item.

## Plain-language legal baseline (first)

China is not an at-will termination environment. Employer-initiated termination should fit a lawful route under the Labor Contract Law framework and be executed with coherent evidence and consistent procedure. For foreign teams, a practical sequence is:

1. Confirm route legality.
2. Confirm evidence integrity.
3. Confirm communication + settlement consistency.
4. Confirm payroll + local implementation readiness.
5. Confirm closure evidence readiness.

State Council implementation regulations and SPC labor-dispute interpretation matter because disputes often test consistency across these controls.

## Pulse preflight card: go/no-go checks

### Check A — Route clearance

**Go condition:** one route rationale, consistent across legal/HR/manager artifacts.  
**No-go trigger:** route drift or unsupported rationale.  
**Action:** stop progression; reissue canonical route note.

### Check B — Evidence clearance

**Go condition:** chronology coherent, ownership clear, versions controlled.  
**No-go trigger:** contradiction, gap, or source ambiguity.  
**Action:** freeze communication until reconciled.

### Check C — Communication clearance

**Go condition:** approved script version matches planned delivery.  
**No-go trigger:** script drift or informal edits.  
**Action:** lock script and apply exception approval path.

### Check D — Settlement clearance

**Go condition:** legal settlement terms match payroll mapping and manager explanation.  
**No-go trigger:** cross-channel mismatch.  
**Action:** enforce single settlement source sheet + revalidation.

### Check E — Payroll lock clearance

**Go condition:** treatment assumptions validated before lock.  
**No-go trigger:** unresolved logic near lock window.  
**Action:** trigger legal-payroll stop/go gate.

### Check F — Local clearance

**Go condition:** city-level validation completed and recorded.  
**No-go trigger:** unresolved local caveat.  
**Action:** hold release until local sign-off.

### Check G — Closure clearance

**Go condition:** payment proof + archive checklist complete.  
**No-go trigger:** closure marked without evidence-complete pack.  
**Action:** block closure state change.

## Practical implications for foreign employers

The preflight model is useful for non-China leadership because it removes ambiguous status language. A case either clears a check or it does not. That makes intervention timing faster and accountability cleaner.

Operationally, this reduces late rework around communication corrections, payroll lock reversals, and post-signature disputes. For cross-border teams managing multiple entities, preflight discipline also improves consistency across locations.

## Daily 20-minute preflight review

### Minute 0-5: route + evidence
- Review Checks A-B for active cases.
- Stop any case with unresolved no-go triggers.

### Minute 5-10: communication + settlement
- Validate Checks C-D.
- Confirm script/source-sheet version integrity.

### Minute 10-15: payroll + local
- Validate Checks E-F before any release.
- Apply stop/go where needed.

### Minute 15-20: closure + leadership brief
- Review Check G on near-closure cases.
- Publish no-go list with owner + ETA.

## SOP by role

### HR
1. Maintain preflight board and owner map.
2. Block progression on no-go conditions.
3. Capture employee communication records.

### Legal
1. Approve route rationale and core wording.
2. Validate settlement legal consistency.
3. Co-sign high-risk stop/go decisions.

### Manager
1. Provide date-stamped factual updates.
2. Use approved script only.
3. Escalate material new facts immediately.

### Payroll/Finance
1. Validate settlement mapping and treatment assumptions.
2. Enforce pre-lock Check E.
3. Produce payment proof for closure evidence.

### Compliance/Ops
1. Audit check transitions and reason codes.
2. Track repeated no-go patterns by city/team.
3. Verify final archive completeness.

## Required document checklist

- Canonical route note
- Evidence chronology log
- Approved script version
- Settlement source sheet
- Payroll treatment mapping
- Local validation memo
- Payment proof + closure archive checklist

SLA baseline:
- route note within 1 business day,
- chronology pass within 2 business days,
- settlement/payroll sync before lock,
- closure archive within 2 business days after payment.

## Exception playbook

### No-go at Check A (route)
- Stop progression.
- Reassess route against updated facts.
- Reissue route note before restart.

### No-go at Check B (evidence)
- Freeze communication.
- Resolve chronology/ownership conflicts.
- Resume only after verified pass.

### No-go at Check D (settlement)
- Use documented clarification path.
- Reconcile legal text and payroll mapping.
- Reapprove communication language.

### No-go at Check F (local)
- Hold release.
- Confirm city-level execution feasibility.
- Update release plan from local outcome.

### No-go at Check E (payroll)
- Trigger stop/go before lock.
- Correct mapping and revalidate.
- Align employee-facing explanation.

## Common mistakes and prevention

### Mistake: launching with “mostly ready” status
**Prevention:** enforce binary go/no-go checks.

### Mistake: multiple active source documents
**Prevention:** one source document per artifact class.

### Mistake: progress updates without blockers
**Prevention:** include no-go check + owner + ETA in each update.

### Mistake: local validation treated as optional
**Prevention:** keep Check F blocking by design.

### Mistake: closure marked before evidence complete
**Prevention:** require Check G pass before closure.

## FAQ

### 1) Is this legal advice?
No. This is an operations governance brief for decision support.

### 2) Can unilateral routes still be used?
Potentially yes, where legal/factual conditions support them; evidence quality is critical.

### 3) Why include payroll before final legal close?
Because settlement treatment alignment affects both compliance risk and employee trust outcomes.

### 4) Is negotiated termination always lower risk?
No. It helps in many cases but still requires coherent execution.

### 5) What KPI should leadership start with?
Track **preflight go-rate at release gates** and **no-go resolution time**.
