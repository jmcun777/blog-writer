# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Executive Scorecard Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- Foreign employers should evaluate China termination readiness with a scorecard, not intuition.
- The highest-value metrics are route integrity, payroll lock quality, script alignment, and closure retrievability.
- If one core metric drops below threshold, executive hold is often cheaper than forced acceleration.
- Scorecard discipline improves both speed and defensibility over time.
- This is a Pulse-style impact brief; legal depth is secondary in the appendix.

## Why this matters now
Cross-border teams often rely on qualitative updates (“looks okay,” “mostly ready”). That language is risky under pressure. A scorecard forces clarity: what is strong, what is weak, and what must be fixed before communication.

This run rotates to an **executive scorecard format** designed for foreign leadership teams who need fast, repeatable decisions across multiple active cases.

## Plain-language legal context first
China termination execution must stay consistent across legal route, facts, payout/tax treatment, and communication behavior. When one layer diverges, dispute and rework risk increase.

National law sets the framework, but city-level practice can materially change outcomes. Scorecards should therefore include locality-sensitive confidence signals.

## The 5-metric executive scorecard
Score each metric 0-20 (total 100):

### Metric 1 — Route integrity (0-20)
- Are route assumptions current and contradiction-managed?
- Is locality-sensitive interpretation captured?

### Metric 2 — Evidence integrity (0-20)
- Can chronology be reconstructed without gaps?
- Are source records versioned and attributable?

### Metric 3 — Payroll/IIT integrity (0-20)
- Is there one locked worksheet with documented tax assumptions?
- Are approvals complete and auditable?

### Metric 4 — Communication integrity (0-20)
- Are scripts linked to latest route and worksheet versions?
- Is spokesperson escalation language current?

### Metric 5 — Closure integrity (0-20)
- Can final rationale and payout trail be retrieved quickly?
- Has retrieval testing passed?

## Decision thresholds
- **85-100:** proceed with normal monitoring.
- **70-84:** conditional proceed with written mitigations and deadlines.
- **<70:** hold and remediate before communication.

Also apply hard-stop overrides: unresolved contradiction, active worksheet conflict, or script mismatch.

## Why this scorecard works for foreign teams
It translates legal and operational complexity into one comparable scale. Regional leaders can see where risk concentrates without replacing local legal judgment. It also improves cross-functional behavior: teams optimize for measurable quality, not presentation quality.

## Practical snapshots

### Snapshot A: high route score, low payroll score
Case looks legally sound but worksheet integrity is weak.

**Scorecard implication:** do not communicate; remediate payroll lock first.

### Snapshot B: strong operational scores, weak closure score
Execution is fast but record retrieval fails.

**Scorecard implication:** closure not defensible; reopen close controls.

### Snapshot C: mid-range all metrics under time pressure
No single crisis, but cumulative risk is high.

**Scorecard implication:** conditional proceed only with explicit mitigation and executive owner.

## Weekly scorecard review SOP
1. Re-score all active high-risk cases.
2. Highlight biggest downward metric movement.
3. Assign one owner per low metric.
4. Set next review deadline and expected score target.
5. Escalate any hard-stop override immediately.

## Implementation checklist
- Standard score template adopted
- Named owner for each metric
- Hard-stop override policy approved
- Score-to-action rules documented
- Retrieval test required at closure

## 14-day rollout plan
**Days 1-3:** establish baseline scores for active cases.

**Days 4-6:** set hard-stop rules and owner accountability.

**Days 7-10:** remediate lowest-scoring metric per case.

**Days 11-14:** rerun scores and compare improvement delta.

## KPI set for leadership
- Average total score by week
- % cases below 70
- Hard-stop override count
- Post-communication correction rate
- Retrieval-test pass rate

## Scorecard governance rules (must-have)
1. No score older than 5 business days can be used for go-live decisions.
2. Any metric below 12/20 requires a named remediation owner and date.
3. Cases with two or more metrics below 12/20 cannot proceed to communication.
4. Any hard-stop override requires executive sign-off with residual risk note.

## 7-day score uplift sprint
**Day 1:** rank active cases by lowest metric.
**Day 2:** prioritize route and payroll score repairs first.
**Day 3:** enforce script-to-worksheet version linkage.
**Day 4:** clear aged blocker items >48h.
**Day 5:** run closure retrieval tests on recently closed cases.
**Day 6:** rerun scorecard and compute delta.
**Day 7:** publish improvement memo and next-week focus list.

## Scorecard failure patterns and fixes
### Pattern 1: High route score, low payroll score
Teams over-index on legal confidence while payout assumptions are still unstable.

**Fix:** force payroll lock before any communication prep and require explicit IIT memo review.

### Pattern 2: High payroll score, low communication score
Numbers are stable but manager scripts lag behind updates.

**Fix:** make script version linkage mandatory and auto-block outdated templates.

### Pattern 3: Strong execution score, weak closure score
Cases are completed operationally but not defensibly.

**Fix:** require retrieval-test evidence as a final close condition.

## Practical board questions by metric drop
When **Route Integrity** drops, ask: “Which assumption changed, and when was it last revalidated?”

When **Evidence Integrity** drops, ask: “Can an independent reviewer reconstruct facts without oral explanation?”

When **Payroll/IIT Integrity** drops, ask: “Why is there more than one active worksheet state?”

When **Communication Integrity** drops, ask: “What prevents old scripts from being used?”

When **Closure Integrity** drops, ask: “Can we answer a challenge in 15 minutes from indexed records alone?”

These questions keep reviews decision-focused rather than narrative-heavy.

## 30-day maturity path
**Week 1:** baseline scores and identify top recurring low metrics.

**Week 2:** implement hard dependency controls and owner accountability.

**Week 3:** run cross-functional calibration to align scoring standards.

**Week 4:** publish trendline, defect recurrence map, and control update plan.

A maturity path helps avoid one-off fixes and supports sustained quality improvements.

## What to watch next
- Whether payroll integrity remains the lowest metric.
- Whether hard-stop overrides decline over two cycles.
- Whether closure score improves after retrieval gating.

## FAQ
### 1) Why score if we already have legal review?
Scorecards make cross-functional execution quality visible and comparable.

### 2) Can we proceed below 85?
Yes, conditionally, but with written mitigations and tight review windows.

### 3) What should never be overridden?
Active contradiction, worksheet conflict, or script mismatch.

### 4) Who owns final score?
A designated case owner with legal/payroll/comms co-sign.

### 5) Is this legal advice?
No. It is operational governance guidance.

### 6) What’s the fastest win?
Improve payroll and script version linkage discipline.
