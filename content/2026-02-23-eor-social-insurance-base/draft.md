# China Social Insurance Contribution Base Resets: Payroll Controls for EOR Operations

## Executive summary
- Social insurance contribution bases are regularly adjusted at local level.
- Base reset periods can create sudden payroll cost changes.
- EOR clients should align budgeting, contracts, and employee communication in advance.

## What changed
Local authorities periodically publish contribution base ranges and implementation windows. When a reset starts, both employer and employee contribution amounts can change even when gross salary stays the same.

## Why it matters for foreign employers / EOR buyers
For global teams entering China, social insurance base management is a key operational risk area:
- inaccurate bases may trigger arrears and penalties,
- employee take-home pay may shift unexpectedly,
- finance forecasts can drift if reset timing is missed.

## What to do now
- Maintain a calendar of local base-reset windows by city.
- Run payroll simulation before implementation month.
- Prepare employee notices explaining net-pay impact.
- Verify EOR audit logs for base changes and approvals.

## FAQ
### Are social insurance bases the same across China?
No. They are administered locally and can differ by region.

### Can we keep old contribution bases for convenience?
Generally no. Employers should follow current local implementation rules.

### Is housing fund handled the same way?
Housing fund has separate local rules and should be checked independently.
