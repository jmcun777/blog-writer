# 2026 Risk Control Framework: Social Insurance for Foreign Employees in China

## Key takeaways
- Most social insurance risk is execution risk.
- A risk framework should map legal requirement -> operational control -> evidence artifact.
- Foreign employers need city-aware governance, not one-size-fits-all handling.

## Risk map
### Risk 1: Wrong enrollment assumption
Control: mandatory employee category decision gate.
Evidence: decision log with approver and date.

### Risk 2: Treaty exemption overuse
Control: exemption only after certificate validation.
Evidence: certificate copy + local acceptance confirmation.

### Risk 3: Payroll mismatch
Control: pre-run simulation + post-run reconciliation.
Evidence: simulation report + reconciliation delta report.

## Operating model
- **Policy layer:** legal interpretation baseline.
- **Execution layer:** HR/payroll checklist and SLA.
- **Assurance layer:** monthly internal review and exception dashboard.

## Implementation blueprint (30-day)
- Week 1: define field model and owner matrix.
- Week 2: deploy SOP and exception queue.
- Week 3: run pilot payroll with dual review.
- Week 4: audit evidence quality and close gaps.

## Exception handling standard
- Severity A (legal ambiguity): compliance owner + legal review.
- Severity B (doc delay): provisional path + next-cycle correction.
- Severity C (employee query): standardized response pack.
