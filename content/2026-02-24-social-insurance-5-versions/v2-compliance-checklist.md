# China Social Insurance for Foreign Employees: 2026 Compliance Checklist for In-House Teams

## Key takeaways
- Policy is important, but checklist discipline determines real compliance outcomes.
- Teams should run enrollment and contribution as a recurring control cycle.
- Treaty treatment should be managed as an exception workflow, not a default assumption.

## Quick compliance checklist
1. Employee classification confirmed.
2. City policy parameters loaded.
3. Mandatory documents complete.
4. Treaty certificate verified (if claimed).
5. Payroll simulation reviewed.
6. Employee deduction notice sent.
7. Filing evidence archived.

## Process design for monthly operations
Use a two-track model:
- **Standard track:** full contribution workflow.
- **Exception track:** treaty/late-doc/dispute cases with higher approval level.

## Role ownership matrix
- HR: onboarding docs, category validation, employee notice.
- Payroll: base/rate calculation, net-pay impact, reconciliation.
- Compliance/Legal: treaty interpretation and high-risk case review.
- Vendor/EOR: filing execution and receipt evidence.

## Control points by date
- T-7: check employee + city parameter integrity.
- T-3: lock contribution assumptions and route exceptions.
- T-1: send final payroll impact communication.
- T+1: reconcile expected vs actual contribution output.

## Exception scenarios
- Certificate pending: keep standard contribution path.
- Local acceptance uncertain: require formal confirmation artifact.
- Disputed treatment: open case ticket with response SLA <48h.

## Common operational failures
- Fragmented data ownership between HR and payroll.
- No treaty-document expiry monitoring.
- Missing evidence links between decision and filing result.
