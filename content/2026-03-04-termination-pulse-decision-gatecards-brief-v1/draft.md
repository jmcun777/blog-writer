# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Decision-Gatecards Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- Use decision gatecards to enforce hard yes/no checks before progression.
- For foreign employers, Gate 1-3 (route, evidence, payroll) should be treated as hard blockers.
- Legal context comes first; practical gate decisions come immediately after.

## Plain-language legal context first
China is not at-will. Employer-initiated termination generally requires a lawful statutory basis under the Labor Contract Law framework, supported by coherent evidence and procedural consistency. Core anchors: Labor Contract Law, Decree No. 535 implementation rules, and SPC labor-dispute interpretation.

## Pulse decision gatecards

### Gatecard 1 — Route legality coherence
**Question:** Is one lawful route consistently documented?  
**Pass rule:** Single canonical route memo exists.  
**Fail action:** Immediate hold on employee-facing steps.

### Gatecard 2 — Evidence chronology integrity
**Question:** Are chronology/version records complete and contradiction-free?  
**Pass rule:** No unresolved record conflicts.  
**Fail action:** Pause progression until reconciled.

### Gatecard 3 — Settlement/payroll/tax mapping
**Question:** Do terms and payroll treatment fully align pre-lock?  
**Pass rule:** Legal-payroll co-sign complete.  
**Fail action:** No-go before payroll lock.

### Gatecard 4 — Communication discipline
**Question:** Are manager/HR scripts aligned and acknowledged?  
**Pass rule:** Approved script confirmation logged.  
**Fail action:** Re-brief and monitor; escalate if repeated.

### Gatecard 5 — Local interpretation validation
**Question:** Is city-level validation complete?  
**Pass rule:** Local memo logged for current case.  
**Fail action:** Block final action.

### Gatecard 6 — Closure evidence completeness
**Question:** Is payment proof + archive packet complete?  
**Pass rule:** Closure packet completeness pass.  
**Fail action:** Keep case open.

## Why this helps foreign employers
Gatecards make complex local compliance executable for non-local teams. Each checkpoint has a binary decision rule and a predefined fail action, reducing ambiguity in cross-time-zone handoffs.

## What improved vs previous run
Compared with exception-ladder format, this run improves operational precision by converting escalation tiers into explicit gate questions with deterministic pass/fail rules.

## Minimum documentation controls
- Canonical route memo
- Versioned chronology log
- Script acknowledgment record
- Settlement mapping sheet
- Payroll/tax reconciliation sheet
- City-level validation memo
- Closure evidence packet
