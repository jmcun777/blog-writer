# Labor Dispute Risk in China: Prevention Controls for EOR and Global HR Teams

## Executive summary
- Most labor disputes are preventable through better contract, attendance, and evidence controls.
- Payroll and HR process quality is often decisive in arbitration outcomes.
- EOR buyers should evaluate documentation discipline, not only service pricing.

## What changed
Recent judicial communication and labor arbitration practice continue emphasizing evidence quality: contract terms, policy acknowledgment, attendance records, and payroll traceability.

## Why it matters for foreign employers / EOR buyers
When companies scale quickly in China, weak process controls can increase dispute probability around overtime, termination, variable pay, and policy enforcement.

## What to do now
- Standardize bilingual contract and handbook acknowledgment workflow.
- Keep immutable attendance and approval records.
- Ensure payroll items are clearly labeled and reconcilable.
- Require EOR monthly compliance review notes for high-risk cases.

## FAQ
### Is labor arbitration always employee-favorable?
Outcomes depend heavily on facts and evidence quality.

### Can email approvals replace signed policy acknowledgment?
It depends on context; stronger evidence frameworks reduce uncertainty.

### What is the first control to fix?
Contract and policy documentation consistency is usually the highest-return fix.
