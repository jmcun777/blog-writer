# China Employee Termination in 2026: Pulse Watchlist Brief for Foreign Employers

## Key takeaways
- Use a watchlist model to keep high-risk termination controls visible every day.
- Route/evidence/payroll belong on the red watchlist for immediate intervention.
- Start with legal context, then move directly into practical monitoring actions.

## Plain-language legal context first
China is not an at-will system. Employer-initiated termination generally requires a lawful statutory route and coherent process evidence under the Labor Contract Law framework. Core anchors: Labor Contract Law, Decree No. 535 implementation rules, and SPC labor-dispute interpretation.

## Pulse watchlist board

### Red watch (immediate action)
- Route rationale inconsistency
- Evidence chronology contradictions
- Settlement/payroll/tax mapping mismatch

**Rule:** Any red-watch item = no-go until resolved.

### Amber watch (active follow-up)
- Manager script drift
- Missing city-level validation memo

**Rule:** Promote to red if unresolved near employee communication or payroll lock.

### Green watch (maintain integrity)
- Closure packet assembly in progress
- Archived payment proof confirmed

**Rule:** Case cannot close until green-watch closeout checklist passes.

## Why this helps foreign employers
A watchlist gives non-local teams a compact risk view that is easy to hand off across time zones: what is blocking now, what needs active follow-up, and what is ready to close.

## What improved vs previous run
Compared with handoff-protocol framing, this run improves ongoing visibility by consolidating cross-functional risks into a daily red/amber/green monitoring model.

## Minimum documentation controls
- Canonical route memo
- Versioned chronology log
- Script acknowledgment record
- Settlement mapping sheet
- Payroll/tax reconciliation sheet
- City-level validation memo
- Closure evidence packet
