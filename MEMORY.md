## Writing Preferences (Permanent)
For expert-style blog writing, user wants:
- Clear stance and judgment first; avoid neutral/hedging framing.
- High information density and industry-native language; avoid basic tutorial tone.
- Decision logic in every piece: why this approach, key risks, trade-offs, who it fits/doesn’t fit.
- Tacit knowledge and real operational context (not just public-info remix).
- Tone: calm, objective, logic-driven; avoid pleasing/flattering language.
- Avoid repetitive templates (especially rigid timeline and formulaic rhythm).
- Include conflict, constraints, and boundaries (applicable vs non-applicable cases).
- Reduce “AI flavor”: less symmetry/mechanical pros-cons; vary rhythm and structure.
- Target output type: industry insight, business decision analysis, structural breakdown, risk alerts, model comparison (not mass-market explainer).
- Completion bar: clear core view + decision path + realistic scenarios + risk boundaries + net-new insight.
- For foreign clients unfamiliar with China, prioritize introductory legal/regulatory explanation first; keep deeply time-sensitive operational tactics secondary.
- User wants output style to benchmark against ChatGPT Pulse daily briefs; target strong similarity in briefing flow while preserving factual rigor.
- User prefers receiving article links only after online URL is confirmed reachable (HTTP 200), to avoid 404 links.
- If the same error occurs in 2 consecutive runs, auto-attempt remediation immediately (without waiting for user instruction), then report fix status.
