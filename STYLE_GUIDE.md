# Editorial Style Guide (China Briefing-inspired)

Target reference style: policy-explainer writing for international business readers.

## Voice
- Senior analyst tone: neutral, practical, evidence-led.
- Explain legal context in business language (not academic legal prose).
- Avoid hype, avoid clickbait exaggeration.

## Article Structure
1. Hook paragraph: what changed + why a foreign employer should care now.
2. Key takeaways box (3-5 bullets).
3. Background context and regulatory framing.
4. Business impact analysis (cost, risk, timeline, operations).
5. Practical action checklist for employers.
6. FAQ (decision-oriented, concise answers).
7. Sources and date stamps.

## Readability
- Paragraphs should usually be 90-170 words.
- Keep sentences crisp; define China-specific terms once.
- Use transitional lines between sections.

## Credibility Markers
- Distinguish confirmed facts vs interpretive risk.
- Include “what to watch next” where rules may evolve.
- Provide source links and update date.

## SEO + CTR
- Use specific, plain-English titles foreign operators would search.
- Front-load intent terms: China, payroll, labor law, compliance, employer.
- Prefer utility headlines over vague commentary.
